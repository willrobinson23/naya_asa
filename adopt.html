Overview of Adoption Process In Nepal

Adopting a child in Nepal involves several legal steps and requirements. The adoption process is designed to protect the welfare of the child and ensure that prospective parents are capable of providing a safe and nurturing environment.

Eligibility Criteria

For Married Couples: Must be married for at least 10 years without children. Both spouses must consent to the adoption.
For Single Individuals: Must be at least 45 years old, unmarried, widowed, or divorced with no children.
Financial Stability: Prospective parents must demonstrate financial capacity to support the child.

Take These Steps

1

Research and Preparation

Start by researching adoption laws and agencies in Nepal. Ensure you understand the differences between domestic and international adoptions, as well as the costs involved.

2

Contact the Central Authority

In Nepal, the Ministry of Women, Children, and Senior Citizens (MOWCSC) handles adoption matters. You'll need to submit an application to the Intercountry Adoption Board (ICAB) under this ministry.

3

Home Study

A home study will be conducted by an accredited agency to evaluate your suitability as adoptive parents. This includes interviews, home visits, and background checks.

4

Application and Matching

Submit the necessary documents, including a guarantee letter confirming that you are eligible to adopt. After submission, the Central Child Welfare Board (CCWB) or the Nepal Children's Organization will match you with a child.

5

Adoption Process and Court Approval

After a child is matched with you, the adoption is processed by the MOWCSC. Although the process does not involve the court, the ministry's approval is mandatory.

6

Finalization and Bringing the Child Home

Once approved, you must finalize the adoption by completing the legal formalities and paying any fees. The process from application to finalization usually takes about three months, but delays may occur.

Fill Out the Inquiry Form
